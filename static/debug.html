How to read this: the dot is the horizontal direction of magnetic north in the board frame; the trail accumulates as you rotate. With good calibration it traces a circle centered on the crosshair. An off-center circle = bad hard-iron offsets (press RESET MAG CAL and slowly rotate a full turn away from the laptop/metal). A wildly varying |B| total means magnetic interference nearby.

Cardinal test: hold the board flat and point its logo/USB edge north, then east, south, west. The tilt-compensated heading should read ~000°, 090°, 180°, 270°. If east/west are swapped (090 ↔ 270), the mirror/handedness is wrong — report that.
